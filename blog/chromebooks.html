Chromebooks, and fun with them

In the context of a school issued Chromebook


Intro

I think Chromebooks are kinda good because you can get a useable experience on very cheap hardware. Sure you can't do much but nowadays a lot of people spend 90% of their time in the browser. The problem is that other 10%. Chromebooks are slowly filling that with android and Linux apps. I was able to do coding and shit with atom and python in the linux container. Some apps still don't work great/at all tho

Doing more with Chromebooks

As to 'hacking' them. You may not need to, Linux apps may be enough for you and the school may not have locked them down. You can also dualboot Chromebooks, or boot from USB sticks, but only if you've installed a custom BIOS, which you need to enable developer mode for. Idk if the school can stop u from doing that in software, if they can they probably will have, and its very obvious that you've enabled developer mode if you do (big warning screen when you boot it with instructions on how to wipe it and return it to normal). You might be able to disbale this if you disable write protection, which you do by removing a screw on the motherboard.

Now onto what distro to run. A lot of drivers for your Chromebook are probably missing from Linux. Gallium OS is likely to have better support for your Chromebook. I hated xfce that came with gallium and xfce+gnome took up too much space on the emmc storage. I eventually got mint working fairly well with the gallium os kernel. Its also possible that some Chromebook drivers are being ported to mainline so using the latest mainline kernel rather than mints may help too. I know some people also had success with arch, idk if this is because they had more control, or the latest mainline, or a kernel with the drivers, but idk.

This all also depends on your model of Chromebook. Mr Chromeboxes website is good to check, as well as the gallium os wiki. There are also some good subreddits, like r/chrultrabook iirc.
